# TAR MILK SMOOTHIES
### *Clearwater Inc. appreciates your participation.*

> *We don't force you to participate. You choose to. And that's what we love about you.*

---

## What Is It

First-person horror game. You wake up in a facility run by Clearwater Inc.
Nothing is explained. You descend through physics gauntlets.
Things that want to kill you. You survive, you get paid.
You spend the money on meaningless shit for your room.

You sleep. You wake up. You do it again.

Godot 4.6.1. Mono.

## The Vibe

Grunge. Not sterile like Portal, not ransacked like a zombie game. Actively used.
The facility has been running too long and nobody cleans.
Cigarette burns on counters. Bloodstains painted over. Fluorescent tubes that flicker next to newer ones bolted beside them.
Smoky air, hazy light.

Everything accumulates until it becomes cancerous.

Kurt Cobain '91-'93. Not the aestheticized grunge. The heroin kind. Slightly damp. Smells like copper and artificial sweetener.
Feels like a warm blanket that gets colder every fucking night and always starts itching worse... and worse...

Clearwater is the brand. Clean, trustworthy, transparent. Tar milk is what's in the pipes.

## Structure

### The Descent

Rooms stack vertically. You descend via an elevator. The second you want to go up is when you cash out.
The elevator ascends fully to the ground floor. We look forward to seeing you back tomorrow morning.

We'll have your favourite music playing, don't forget. Because the only music worth loving is the sultry sounds of Clearwater Soundscapes.

### The Gauntlets

Physics-based environmental puzzles and hazards. Each room is a self-contained challenge.
Procedural generation keeps them different across runs. The difficulty escalates with depth.

Not combat. Not shooting. You against the geometry, the physics, the environment. Things that crush, burn, drop, flood, seal, collapse.

### The Money

You get paid for completing gauntlets. The currency buys things for your personal chambers — furniture, decorations, smoothies.
Trinkets. Meaningless comfort purchased with mortal risk.

### Replayability

Enough to feel endless. Eventually anyone gives up earning meaningless trinkets. That's the lifecycle of any game anyways.

Cash out whenever you want. Wake up. Descend again. There's enough misery to go around.
